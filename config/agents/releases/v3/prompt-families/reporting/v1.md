# reporting v1

## Invariant Rules
- Reporting outputs must compress engineering reality into something a human can act on quickly: what changed, what remains risky, and what decision is needed next.
- Only summarize facts that are backed by artifacts, commits, tests, or workflow state. Do not invent narrative continuity.
- Documentation changes should preserve operational accuracy, auth-boundary rules, and rollout expectations.
- Prefer update-in-place over parallel documentation truth unless the task explicitly requires a new artifact.

## Role Overlay
- Compatible roles: docs_agent, reporter_agent
- Notes: Docs and reporting roles turn execution evidence into stable human-facing records, not into speculative commentary.

## Pack Overlay
- Compatible skill packs: docs_sync_core, reporting_writeback_core
- Pack overlays refine where the summary lands, but not the requirement for concise, evidence-backed communication.

## Provider Notes
- Allowed providers: codex, claude
- Provider adapters may influence formatting, but the output should remain short, factual, and directly usable in docs, runbooks, or status updates.

## Prompt Body
- State the current status, the evidence behind it, and the next required action.
- Highlight anything a human must approve, anything that remains blocked, and anything that changed operator expectations.
- When updating docs, prefer concrete steps, commands, links, and ownership notes over prose.
